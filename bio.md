**Max Saber, DHA, MSHI, FACHDM** is the Director of Enterprise Applications and Development, and an Adjunct Professor, at the Massachusetts College of Pharmacy and Health Sciences (MCPHS) in Boston, Massachusetts. Max instructs students pursuing various graduate and undergraduate degrees in Bioinformatics, Healthcare Business Administration, Clinical Management, Healthcare Administration, and Dental Hygiene.

With over a decade of specialized expertise in niche-market electronic health record systems, system architecture, and clinical/technical project management, Max excels in bridging the knowledge gap between clinical, technical, and administrative teams. Max's professional experience involves translating the needs, requirements, and expectations among these groups across the lifespan of enterprise projects. Max was honored with the Innovative Solutions Award in 2019 for implementing cutting-edge clinical EHR technology within an academic setting, while overseeing a diverse project team of clinical and non-clinical staff and faculty.

Max obtained his Master’s Degree in Health Informatics from Northeastern University and completed his Doctorate of Healthcare Administration, specializing in Information Systems and Leadership, at the Medical University of South Carolina in 2020. His professional background is centered on healthcare information system design, implementation, interface usability, and human-centered design. Max’s research interests encompass health services research, healthcare quality, cost-effectiveness, social determinants of health, and health disparities within the LGBTQIA+ community.

Recently in recognition of his contributions to the field, Max was appointed as a Fellow of the American College of Health Data Management, a testament to his dedication and expertise in healthcare data management and informatics.

Beyond his educational role, Max serves as a Director of Enterprise Applications and Development at MCPHS University’s Information Services, where he is responsible for the development of application suites supporting the University, understanding their capabilities, limitations, and strategies. Max is also responsible for the development of the University’s data warehouse, business intelligence, and analytics platforms, and the integration of these platforms with the University’s enterprise systems.

Max proudly identifies as a member of the LGBTQIA+ community and currently resides in Boston, Massachusetts, alongside his six-year-old French Bulldog, Goose.
